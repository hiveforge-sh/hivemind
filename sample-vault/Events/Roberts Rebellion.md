# Robert's Rebellion

## Summary

A civil war that ended the Targaryen dynasty and placed Robert Baratheon on the Iron Throne. The war began after Prince Rhaegar Targaryen allegedly abducted Lyanna Stark, and King Aerys II executed Lord Rickard Stark and his heir Brandon.

## Participants

- [[Eddard Stark]] - Led northern forces alongside Robert
- [[Robert Baratheon]] - Leader of the rebellion, betrothed to Lyanna Stark

## Timeline

- **282 AC**: War begins after Lyanna Stark's disappearance and the execution of Rickard and Brandon Stark
- **283 AC**: Battle of the Trident - Robert slays Rhaegar Targaryen
- **283 AC**: Sack of King's Landing - Jaime Lannister kills the Mad King

## Outcome

Robert Baratheon crowned King of the Seven Kingdoms. The Targaryen children flee to Essos.

## Long-term Impact

- End of 300 years of Targaryen rule
- New political alliances formed through marriage
- Seeds of future conflict planted

## Notes

One of the defining events of recent Westerosi history. The true circumstances of Lyanna's "abduction" remain disputed.
